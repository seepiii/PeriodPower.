                     Breaking barriers, menstrual equity for all

Period poverty is a saddening, yet prevalent issue in this modern day world. It is the lack of access to sanitary products, education, and facilities during menstruation due to financial constraints and/or social stigma. According to The World Bank, 500 million people lack access to menstrual products and hygiene facilities around the world. Menstrual products should be a basic right for people that experience periods.

This week's memoir :

"Growing up in a low-income family, I never gave much thought to menstrual products. I knew they were expensive and we couldn't afford them, so I just used old rags and hoped for the best. It wasn't until I was in high school that I realized the true impact of period poverty.

I remember one day, I bled through my makeshift pad during class. I was mortified and couldn't focus on anything else for the rest of the day. That's when I knew something had to change.

I started researching period poverty and found out that it was a widespread issue, affecting not just me but countless others in my community. I decided to take action and started a menstrual product drive at my school. I collected donations of pads and tampons, and distributed them to low-income students in my school.

It was a small step, but it made a huge impact. Seeing the relief on my classmates' faces as they received the products they needed was truly heartwarming. I knew I had to do more.

I started working with local organizations to raise awareness about period poverty and advocate for menstrual equity. I also started a scholarship to help low-income students afford menstrual products.

It's been a few years since I graduated, but I'm still passionate about fighting period poverty. I know that by working together, we can make sure that no one has to suffer through their menstrual cycle alone."

-Anonymous
