Period Poverty

Period poverty is a serious and pressing issue that affects millions of people around the world. Despite the fact that menstruation is a natural and normal bodily function, many individuals are unable to access the necessary products, education, and facilities to manage their periods in a safe and hygienic manner. This is particularly prevalent in low-income communities, where financial constraints make it difficult to afford menstrual products, and lack of education and awareness can lead to social stigma and embarrassment surrounding menstruation.

According to The World Bank, an estimated 500 million people lack access to menstrual products and hygiene facilities worldwide. This means that countless individuals are forced to improvise with unsanitary materials, such as rags, leaves, or even ashes, which can lead to serious health complications. Additionally, without access to menstrual products, individuals may miss school or work, which can negatively impact their education and career opportunities.
Furthermore, period poverty is not just a problem that affects developing countries, but it's also prevalent in developed countries like USA, UK and Canada. In these countries, lack of access to menstrual products and education disproportionately impacts low-income individuals, homeless individuals, and individuals experiencing poverty.

It's important to understand that menstrual products should be considered a basic right for people who experience periods. It's not a luxury, but a necessity. Governments, NGOs and private sector should come together to make sure that everyone, regardless of their financial situation, has access to menstrual products, education, and facilities to manage their periods in a safe and hygienic way.
